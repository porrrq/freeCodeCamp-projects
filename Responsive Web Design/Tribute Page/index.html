A brief history of ThinkPads

Where durability meets innovation, designed for the business professional.

The evolution of ThinkPad laptops, blending timeless design with cutting-edge technology.

The ThinkPad, a line of laptops initially developed by IBM and later by Lenovo, has a rich history that combines innovation, durability, and iconic design.

The Early Days (1992)

  • Design Origins: The ThinkPad was inspired by a Japanese bento box, emphasizing a minimalist and functional aesthetic. Richard Sapper designed it, and Yamato Labs in Japan played a significant role in its engineering.
  • First Model: The ThinkPad 700C was introduced in October 1992. It featured a 10.4-inch color TFT display and the now-iconic red TrackPoint pointing stick.
  • Name: The name "ThinkPad" was inspired by IBM’s corporate slogan, "Think," and their notepad-like promotional notebooks.

The ThinkPad 700C featured a sturdy build in a relatively compact package.

1990s: Establishing the Legacy

  • The ThinkPad quickly gained popularity for its robust build and reliability.
  • It became the first laptop certified for use on the Space Shuttle, demonstrating its durability in extreme conditions.
  • Key innovations included:
    • The butterfly keyboard in the ThinkPad 701 (1995), which expanded when opened.
    • The introduction of DVD-ROM drives and advanced graphics.

The ThinkPad 701C "Butterfly" is still considered an engineering marvel.

2000s: Transition to Lenovo

  • 2005: Lenovo, a Chinese technology company, acquired IBM's PC division, including the ThinkPad brand.
  • Under Lenovo, the ThinkPad retained its reputation for quality while expanding its market reach.
  • Notable Models:
    • ThinkPad X300 (2008): Ultra-thin and light, often compared to the MacBook Air.
    • ThinkPad T Series: Known for their enterprise-friendly features and performance.

Ultra-thin, ultra-powerful: The ThinkPad X300 (2008) redefined portable computing.

2010s: Modernization and Innovation

  • Lenovo introduced features like carbon fiber materials, touchscreen displays, and 360-degree hinges for Yoga models.
  • ThinkPads remained staples in the business world due to their security features, like the fingerprint scanner and TPM chip.

Light as air, strong as steel: The ThinkPad X1 Carbon (2012) redefines portable power.

2020s and Beyond

  • ThinkPads now cater to a variety of users, from ultrabooks like the X1 Carbon to workstations like the P Series.
  • Sustainability became a focus, with efforts to use recyclable materials and reduce energy consumption.

Transform your workspace: The ThinkPad X1 Yoga(2021) adapts to every need with a 360° hinge and unparalleled performance.

Cultural Impact

The ThinkPad is often considered a symbol of productivity and professionalism. Its loyal following appreciates its no-nonsense design and focus on functionality over trends.

Legacy

With over three decades of continuous innovation, the ThinkPad remains one of the most respected laptop brands in the world, bridging the gap between traditional reliability and modern computing needs.
